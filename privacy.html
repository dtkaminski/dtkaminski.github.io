Privacy Policy

Last updated: 12 June 2026
This is the privacy policy for the Operator Intelligence beta. It describes our practices accurately as the product stands today. It is written to be honest rather than exhaustive and should be reviewed by a solicitor before general availability. Questions: contact us.

1. Who we are

Operator Intelligence ("we", "us") provides an analytics and operations platform for direct-to-consumer (DTC) brands. For the personal data we process about your customers, you (the brand) are the data controller and we act as your data processor. For the personal data we hold about you, the account holder, we are the controller. We are based in the United Kingdom and operate under the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.

All third-party connections request read-only scopes. We never write to, change, or send anything in your store, ad accounts, or email platform.

3. Why we process it (lawful basis)

  • Contract — to provide the analytics, findings, and weekly briefs you sign up for.
  • Legitimate interests — to operate, secure, and improve the service, balanced against your and your customers' rights.

4. Where it's stored and who processes it

Your data is hosted on Supabase (PostgreSQL) in the London (eu-west-2) region. We use the following sub-processors:

  • Supabase — database, authentication, and hosting (UK/EU).
  • Netlify — static site delivery for the marketing site and app shell.
  • Anthropic and/or OpenAI — to generate the natural-language analysis in the "Ask the data" feature and written findings. Only the aggregated figures needed to answer a question are sent; we do not send raw customer records for model training.

Tenant data is isolated per workspace using database row-level security, so one brand cannot access another brand's data.

5. How long we keep it

We retain your data for as long as your workspace is active. If you close your account, we delete your workspace data within 30 days, except where we must retain limited records to meet a legal obligation.

6. Your rights

You can ask us to access, correct, export, or delete personal data we hold. To exercise any of these rights — or to request deletion or export of your customers' data that we process on your behalf — email us via the contact page and we will respond within one month. You also have the right to complain to the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

7. Disconnecting a source

You can revoke any connection at any time from your workspace, or directly in Shopify, Meta, Google, Klaviyo, or TikTok. Revoking stops further syncs; to also delete already-synced data, contact us.

8. Changes

We'll update this page when our practices change and revise the "last updated" date above.
